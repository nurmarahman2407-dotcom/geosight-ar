📦 GeoSight Delivery — Pallet Packing Challenge

Learning Objectives:

  • Connect volume to spatial arrangement
  • Determine the most efficient box size for delivery
  • Justify your choice using mathematical reasoning
The GeoSight Logistics Unit needs to deliver products using a standard pallet.

• Pallet size: 100cm × 100cm
• Current box size: 25cm × 25cm × 20cm
• Boxes can be rotated (e.g., 25×20 on the pallet)

Your task: find the best arrangement so the pallet can carry as many boxes as possible.

Part A — Basic Arrangement

1. The pallet is 100cm × 100cm. The box base is 25cm × 25cm.
a. How many boxes can fit on the pallet if arranged without rotation?

Part B — Rotation Strategy

2. If the box is placed with a 25cm × 20cm base, what is the maximum number of boxes that can fit?

✅ Model Answers

Part A: 100÷25 = 4 boxes across, 4×4 = 16 boxes

Part B: Try 25×20 — 100÷25=4 (x-direction), 100÷20=5 (y-direction) → 4×5 = 20 boxes (better!)

The rotated arrangement (20 boxes) is more efficient than the original arrangement (16 boxes) — it saves more pallet space.
